History of the Rubik's cube

The Rubik's cube was invented by Hungarian professor Ernő Rubik in 1974. At first, he was trying to develop a model for his students so that they could better understand three-dimensional structures. However, after covering the cube with different colors and twisting it, he spent a whole month trying to figure out how to solve it. It was then patented as the "Magic Cube" in 1975. The "Magic Cube" was sold by Ideal Toy Company four years later and was renamed the "Rubik's Cube" in 1980. The Rubik's cube became one of the most popular toys in the world and was featured in various forms of pop culture. Today, it remains to be a frustrating and rewarding puzzle for everybody.
