Fun Facts

  • The Rubik's cube has 43,252,003,274,489,856,000 (approximately 4.3 quintillion) different combinations.
  • It first took Professor Ernő Rubik, the inventor of the Rubik's cube, about a month to solve the cube.
  • The current world record for a three-by-three Rubik's cube single is 4.73 seconds set in December 11, 2016 by Feliks Zemdegs from Australia.
  • Lots of other three-dimensional puzzles, such as the two-by-two, four-by-four, five-by-five, pyraminx, megaminx, mirror cube, etc., are derived from the Rubik's cube.

    A pyraminx in its solved state

  • Because the Rubik's cube symbolizes high intelligence, it is often shown in movies and TV shows such as WALL-E, The Pursuit of Happyness, The Simpsons, etc.

    Rubik's cube in WALL-E

  • Researchers have found out that the hardest combination of the Rubik's cube, the one that requires the most moves, is the superflip.

    The superflip position

  • The maximum ideal number of moves required to solve a cube is 20. This number is often called "God's number."
