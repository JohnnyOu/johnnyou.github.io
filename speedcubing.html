Speedcubing

Speedcubing, also known as speedsolving, is the practice of solving the Rubik's cube or other three-dimensional puzzles as fast as possible. Currently, World Cube Association (WCA) holds many speedcubing events around the world. There are 18 events held by WCA, and they are:

  • Rubik's cube (3x3)
  • 3x3 blindfolded
  • 3x3 one-handed
  • 3x3 fewest moves
  • 3x3 with feet
  • 3x3 multi-blind
  • 2x2 cube
  • 4x4 cube
  • 4x4 blindfolded
  • 5x5 cube
  • 5x5 blindfolded
  • 6x6 cube
  • 7x7 cube
  • Megaminx
  • Pyraminx
  • Square-1
  • Rubik's clock
  • Skewb

Most speedcubers use the CFOP method to solve the Rubik's cube with lots and lots of practice. The 100+ algorithms, finger tricks, and everyday practice altogether make these records possible. In fact, muscle memory plays a key role in performing the algorithms. Practice also can facilitate speedcubers to recognize each situation very quickly. Therefore, it is possible for everybody to become a speedcuber and have a chance to demonstrate his or her skills in a WCA event.
